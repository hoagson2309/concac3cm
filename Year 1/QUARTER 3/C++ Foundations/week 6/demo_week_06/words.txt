boat, a vehicle that can be used to transport goods and people across the water. Comes with a captain or not.
tree, a plant that grows in the ground. Trees have a trunk, branches, and leaves. Some loose leaves periodically.
tulip, a plant that grows from a bulb and has large, brightly colored flowers. Tulips are a national sport in the Netherlands.
umbrella, a device that is used to protect people from the rain. It is made of a long, curved handle and a canopy of fabric. The fabric is hopefully waterproof.
rock, a hard, solid mineral material. Rocks are found in the ground and are used for building things. Rocks are also used in landscaping. Or for throwing.
rocket, a vehicle that is used to travel into space. Rockets are also used in tennis, but those do not travel into space.
mouse, a small mammal with a long tail or a plastic device with a cable. The animal is a rodent and the device is used to control a computer.
mug, a cup with a handle that is used for drinking hot beverages. Must be cleaned periodically to avoid mold.
headphones, a device that produces sound for the ears and is used to protect the wearer from interacting with other people in social situations.
helicopter, a vehicle that can fly in the air by spinning blades. Also a style of parenting that's frowned upon.
mud, a mixture of dirt and water. Children love it, parents, especially of the helicopter variety, do not.
mushroom, a fungus that grows in the ground. Mushrooms are used in cooking and can be poisonous. Do not eat wild mushrooms.
pencil, an analog input device used in conjunction with paper. Requires periodical maintenance with a sharpener.
pencil sharpener, a device used to sharpen pencils. See [pencil].
apple, a fruit that grows on a tree. Commonly found in supermarkets and lunch boxes.
brick, a block of hard material used in building. Clay bricks are for building homes, plastic ones are for playing with.
butterfly, an insect with large, colorful wings. Butterflies also occur in the stomach when one is nervous.
cactus, a plant that grows in the desert. Cacti have sharp spines and store water in their stems. Do not touch.
